Captive portals — the "agree and connect" sign-in pages on airport, hotel, and cafe Wi-Fi — normally open in a browser that shares your device's network. That means the portal, and the network operator behind it, can see requests that should have stayed on your VPN or your encrypted DNS.

Gatepath confines captive-portal sign-in to the captive network itself. It binds the portal WebView to the captive Wi-Fi network with ConnectivityManager.bindProcessToNetwork(), so the sign-in page can reach the portal while your other apps keep using your VPN and private DNS. The portal session is closed when you are done.

When a full-tunnel VPN, private DNS, or an HTTP proxy would block sign-in, Gatepath diagnoses the cause and tells you exactly what to change. Every session is recorded in a local, on-device audit log — never page contents, never credentials.

Free and open source.
